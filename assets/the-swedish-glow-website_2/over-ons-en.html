The story

The real. That's
what it's about.

The story of Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi. Born in Sweden, shaped by the corporate world, psychologist by profession — and a woman who has learned that the body carries what the mind has no time to say.

Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi
Founder · Psychologist
01

Hej, I am Elin.

I am a psychologist. And a founder. But above all I am someone who always wants to talk about what is real — daring to be genuine and honest, even when things aren't going well. That isn't a personality trait for me. It is a choice I make every single day.

The Swedish Glow is the place where both halves are allowed to coexist: the evidence-based and the tangible, the rational and the spiritual. Because that is where women truly live their lives — with facts and intuition, with research and self-care. This brand refuses to make you choose.

Three chapters

A family story, in three movements.

An island. A career. A brand. How a Swedish girl grew into the founder of a family business that now shares the Nordic ritual in 23 countries.

I
My path

From the stable girl, to the boardroom.

Before I became a psychologist, I was an HR manager at ASML, IKEA and Shimano. Before that, in my twenties, I was a leader at H&M. And before that I was a stable girl in Gothenburg who learned to work in the rain and the dark without complaint.

I am the first in my family to graduate in a second language. Seven, eight years I lived within the male hierarchy, the male game, and I was good at it. But I was still, in some way, that same girl from the stables — who had learned to read hierarchies and move within them to make space.

Then came motherhood. And something happened that I cannot explain rationally — and that is precisely the point. For the first time in my life, feeling was stronger than reason. I, who had built my entire life on surviving through hard work and calculation, left a top position at one of the world's largest companies. Not because it was the smart move. But because something inside me was greater than the game I had been playing.

That is where The Swedish Glow begins. Not in a business plan. In a moment where spirituality was stronger than rationality. Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi
II
Sweden

The silence that heals, and the silence that kills.

For generations my family has been successful through physical hard work — and through silence. We have endured difficult times. We did not speak about them. It is a silent contract I was born into.

At home in Sweden are all my loved ones. All the warmth, all the love, all the socialising. And at the same time that is where I have felt most alone. Because it is one thing to be alone in exclusion. It is something entirely different to be alone while belonging — surrounded by people who love you, but who do not have the language for what needs to be said.

The real. That is what I always want to talk about. Daring to be genuine and honest, even when things aren't going well. Not as a personality trait — as a necessity.

You are alone when you are alone from yourself. But solitude is magical when you are at home within yourself. That is the whole difference. Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi
III
2022

A family brand, born from love.

In 2022 I took the leap. Together with my husband I built a brand that brought everything the corporate world had taught me, in a form that feels personal. A Dutch family business with Swedish roots.

The mission is clear: to put women on a pedestal. Not with empty promises, but with products that work. With ingredients of the highest quality. With a ritual that asks for time — and in doing so gives back something that lasts.

Three years later The Swedish Glow has grown into a brand available in 23 countries, with more than 100,000 women who start their morning with us. Still a family brand. Still born from Sweden.

Every bottle we make has first passed through our hands, been tested by us first, first for our family. Only then for yours. Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi
My philosophy

This is what I believe in.

Six convictions that come not from a textbook, but from a life of work, longing, motherhood and silence. The place where the rational and the tangible meet.

01

Glow is not an achievement.

Glow is what remains when you stop building a career on top of an exhausted body. It is not skin. It is a nervous system that has been allowed to breathe.

02

The body carries what the mind does not say.

If you are not allowed to cry, your shoulders weep. If you cannot rest, your stomach collapses. If you are not allowed to take up space, your voice shrinks.

03

Silence is both poison and medicine.

Sweden has taught me both. Loneliness is suffocating when you are not at home within yourself — and liberating once you are. That is the whole difference.

04

Women's health is never neutral.

It is shaped by who cares for whom, who absorbs the blow, who carries loss in silence. Everything settles in the nervous system — not only in the wallet.

05

There is an intelligence greater than the rational.

The female body knows things before we know them. The Swedish Glow is an invitation to listen to that intelligence — not to talk over it.

06

Both halves are allowed to exist.

Evidence and soul. Research and self-care. What can be proven and what can only be felt. Women live with facts and intuition. We refuse to make you choose.

I do not build The Swedish Glow because I am whole. I build it because I have been broken enough times to know what truly holds a woman together — and what ultimately makes her radiate. Elin Hellqvist-Moayedi

In conversation with Elin

Five questions for the founder.

What do you miss most about Brännö?
The silence. You wake up, you make tea, you hear nothing but the sea and perhaps a gull. In the Netherlands it takes a little longer to find that silence, but my morning ritual (a glass of water, a shot of Marine Collagen, a few minutes in the garden) has become my own version of Brännö.
Why make the move from ASML to your own brand?
I learned a great deal in the corporate world. At IKEA, how to build brand and culture. At Shimano, precision. At ASML, scale. But I wanted to build something with my name literally attached to it. A brand where women feel there is someone behind it who truly wants to look them in the eye.
What does "putting women on a pedestal" mean to you?
Three things at once. One: making products so good that a woman feels seen. Two: offering a ritual that makes self-care worthwhile again. No rushing, no guilt, just time for yourself. And three: being the example myself. Female entrepreneur, family mother, and proof that you do not have to choose.
Who else is part of The Swedish Glow?
My husband works alongside me at The Swedish Glow alongside his role in the IT company that he inherited with his brother from his father. Entrepreneurship runs in both families: taking initiative, building something that lasts. Our team is small but passionate, and we work with Swedish producers we now know personally.
Where do you want to be in five years?
I dream of a brand that is in every bathroom in Europe. Not as just another product, but as a trusted ritual. And I dream of a community where women strengthen one another. That for me is what luxury truly means: taking time, choosing quality, and knowing that you are part of something greater than yourself.
